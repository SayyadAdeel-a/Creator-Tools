Web accessibility isn't just good ethics—it's good business. Accessible websites reach more users, rank better in search engines, and often provide better user experiences for everyone. A proper contrast checker is your first line of defense.

### Understanding Contrast Ratios

The WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) defines two levels of contrast compliance:

#### WCAG AA (Minimum)
- Normal text: 4.5:1 ratio
- Large text (18pt+): 3:1 ratio
- UI components: 3:1 ratio

#### WCAG AAA (Enhanced)
- Normal text: 7:1 ratio
- Large text: 4.5:1 ratio

### How to Check Contrast

1. Measure foreground color (text)
2. Measure background color
3. Calculate the ratio using specialized tools
4. Verify compliance with WCAG standards

### Why Contrast Matters

- **Accessibility**: 15% of the world population has some form of visual impairment
- **Legal Compliance**: Many countries require WCAG compliance for websites
- **SEO Benefits**: Accessible sites often perform better in search
- **User Experience**: Better contrast improves readability for all users

### Common Contrast Mistakes

- Light gray text on white backgrounds
- Low-contrast button colors
- Placeholder text that's too light
- Decorative elements that reduce contrast
