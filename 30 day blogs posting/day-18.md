Strong passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access. With data breaches becoming increasingly common, using unique, complex passwords for every account is essential.

### What Makes a Password Strong?

#### Length
Each additional character exponentially increases cracking time. Minimum: 12 characters. Recommended: 16+ characters.

#### Complexity
Include all character types:
- Uppercase letters (A-Z)
- Lowercase letters (a-z)
- Numbers (0-9)
- Special characters (!@#$%^&*)

#### Randomness
Avoid predictable patterns, dictionary words, and personal information.

### Password Strength Guidelines

| Length | Complexity | Crack Time |
|--------|------------|------------|
| 8 | Simple | Seconds |
| 12 | Mixed | Days |
| 16 | Mixed | Years |
| 20 | Mixed | Centuries |

### Password Don'ts

- Never use personal information (birthdays, names)
- Avoid common words and patterns
- Don't reuse passwords across accounts
- Never share passwords via email or chat

### Password Managers

Consider using a password manager to:
- Generate unique passwords
- Store encrypted passwords
- Auto-fill login forms
- Sync across devices
