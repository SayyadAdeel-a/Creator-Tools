A cohesive color palette is the foundation of memorable brand identity. Whether you're building a new brand or refreshing an existing one, understanding how to create harmonious color palettes will elevate your design work.

### Understanding Color Harmony

#### Complementary Colors
Colors opposite on the color wheel. Creates high contrast and vibrant looks:
- Blue + Orange
- Red + Green
- Purple + Yellow

#### Analogous Colors
Colors adjacent on the color wheel. Creates serene, comfortable designs:
- Blue + Green + Teal
- Red + Orange + Yellow

#### Triadic Colors
Three colors equally spaced on the color wheel. Balanced and vibrant:
- Red + Yellow + Blue
- Purple + Orange + Green

### Building Your Brand Palette

#### Step 1: Choose Your Primary Color
Start with your brand's main color. Consider:
- Industry standards (blue = trust, red = energy)
- Target audience preferences
- Unique positioning in your market

#### Step 2: Build Secondary Colors
Select 2-3 supporting colors that complement your primary. Use a 60-30-10 ratio:
- 60% Primary color
- 30% Secondary color(s)
- 10% Accent color

#### Step 3: Define Neutrals
Every palette needs neutral colors:
- White/Off-white
- Dark gray/Black
- Light gray/Beige
